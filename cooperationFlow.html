Short presentation

Cooperation Flow is a tool to visualize any kind of massive cooperation. We call massive cooperation any process in which a large number of agents (usually, individuals) openly publish productions which are based on other agents productions, without much coordination or communication between the agents. For example, article creation in Wikipedia, retweets in Twitter, scientific publications or collective video creation on Nico Nico Douga are typical examples.
For further information, please report to our papers written on the subject, "Understanding mass cooperation through visualization" and "A Visualization Platform For Exploring Cooperation"

Browse an example dataset

As an example, you can browse some typical content creation on Nico Nico Douga (NND).
NND is a video sharing platform, very popular in Japan. We are interested in the cooperative creation of music videos in this platform: some authors create an original music, then another actor sing on this music, another create an illustration, dance, and so on and so forth.

In this visualization, each "flow" corresponds usually to one famous original music, and is composed of all videos partiipating in the cooperation process on this video. The name of the flow is the name of the most characteristic tag associated to the flow.
